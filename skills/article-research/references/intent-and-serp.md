# Intent & SERP Reference

Used in Workflow Steps 2–3 and 5. Always verify intent against the **live** SERP — surface words lie; what actually ranks tells the truth.

## Search intent matrix

| Intent | User goal | Signal words | What ranks | Typical length | Default CTA |
|--------|-----------|--------------|------------|----------------|-------------|
| Informational | Learn / solve | what, how, why, guide, tutorial, examples, steps, checklist, definition | Explainers, tutorials, listicles, FAQs | 800–4,000w by depth | Newsletter, related guide |
| Commercial investigation | Research before buying | best, top, vs, review, alternative, worth it, for [audience], pricing | Comparisons, reviews, ranked roundups, buying guides | 2,000–5,000w | Free trial, demo, comparison |
| Transactional | Complete an action | buy, price, discount, free trial, demo, signup, download, hire, near me | Pricing/landing/signup/local pages | Conversion-focused, short | Buy, subscribe, book |
| Navigational | Reach a known place | brand name, login, pricing, docs, support, official | Homepage, product page, docs | As short as needed | Login, open docs |

### Mixed intent
Pick the **dominant** intent from the SERP, answer that question first, then bridge to the secondary intent naturally and mirror the winning format.

| Query pattern | Dominant | Secondary | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| `best [product] for [use case]` | Commercial | Informational | Buying guide with educational framing |
| `how to [task] with [product]` | Informational | Navigational | Tutorial featuring the product's steps |
| `[product] review and pricing` | Commercial | Transactional | Review with a clear pricing CTA |
| `what is [concept] tools` | Informational | Commercial | Explainer that transitions to options |

### Funnel mapping (sanity-check the angle against where the reader is)
Awareness → Informational · Consideration/Evaluation → Commercial · Decision → Transactional · Retention → Navigational. Match the article's CTA to the stage; don't push "buy now" on an awareness query.

## SERP feature taxonomy (what to record in Step 2)

| Feature | Why it matters | What to capture |
|---|---|---|
| AI Overview | GEO target; shows what AI will cite | Is it present? What sources does it pull? What sub-questions does it answer? |
| Featured snippet | Position-zero target | Format: paragraph (definition) / ordered list (how-to) / table (comparison); current holder |
| People Also Ask (PAA) | Long-tail H2/H3 + GEO questions | **Record verbatim** — these become independently answerable headings |
| Related searches | Adjacent long-tail + cluster spokes | Copy the terms verbatim |
| Video / Image pack | Format expectation | Note if video is required to compete |
| Knowledge panel / Local pack | Entity or local intent signal | Indicates navigational/local intent |
| Sitelinks | Strong brand/navigational signal | Treat the query as navigational if dominant |

**GEO flags** (mark any term that is one of these — they are the most AI-citable): question, definition, comparison, numbered list, how-to. AI engines quote crisp, self-contained answers to these.

## Difficulty scoring (no tool, SERP-only)
Read the top 10 and rate:
- **Easy** — mixed intent, several low-authority/forum/UGC results, thin pages. Realistic: 3–6 months.
- **Moderate** — uniform intent, mostly mid-authority sites, decent depth. Realistic: 6–12 months.
- **Hard** — all top results are high-authority, long-form, highly optimized, possibly with brand dominance. Realistic: 12+ months / authority play — only attempt with a strong evidence-backed angle.

Difficulty informs whether the **Opportunity gap** is worth chasing: a Hard SERP is winnable only where you hold first-party evidence the incumbents lack.

## Target length
Average word count of the top 5 results **+10%**. Never pad to a number; length follows completeness of the answer, not the reverse.
